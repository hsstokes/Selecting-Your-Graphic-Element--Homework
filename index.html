What You Need To Do

1
Look Through Your Existing Pages

Go back through all your design sheets from this project. Look carefully at every page.

You are looking for a small detail that could work as a graphic element ‚Äî something that represents your research theme.

2
Choose One Specific Element

Select one small detail from your pages. This could be:

  • Part of a drawing you made
  • A section of a collage
  • A texture or pattern
  • A small motif or shape
  • Any visual element that is meaningful to your project

Think carefully! This element will play a role in your developing design process.

3
Scan or Photograph the Page

Bring a digital copy of the page that contains your chosen element:

  • Scan as JPEG or PNG
  • Or take a clear photo with good lighting
  • Make sure your chosen detail is clear and visible
  • Save to your phone, USB, or cloud storage

Phone scanning apps: Adobe Scan, Microsoft Lens, or iPhone Notes app

4
Be Ready to Explain Your Choice

In class, you should be able to explain:

  • Which detail you have chosen
  • Why this element is significant to your project
  • How it connects to your research theme

üí≠ Questions to Help You Choose

Ask yourself these questions as you look through your pages:

  • What small detail keeps appearing in my work?
  • Which element best represents my research theme?
  • What shape, texture, or motif feels important to my project?
  • Is there a detail that could work well when repeated or transformed?
  • Which element do I feel most connected to?
